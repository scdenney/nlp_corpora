# Kaebyok Magazine Corpus (1920–1935)

This corpus contains full-text articles from *Kaebyok* (開闢), an influential Korean magazine published during the Japanese colonial period. The magazine served as a major forum for intellectual and cultural debate, covering topics including nationalism, modernization, literature, social reform, and political thought.

---

## Files

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **kaebyok_corpus.csv** | Unified corpus containing all 2,467 articles |

## Variables

| Variable | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **issue_date** | Publication date of the magazine issue (YYYY-MM-DD format) |
| **year** | Publication year (1920–1935) |

---

## Historical Context

*Kaebyok* was one of the most significant Korean-language magazines of the colonial era. First published in 1920, it provided a platform for Korean intellectuals to discuss modernization, national identity, and social change under Japanese rule. The magazine featured essays, fiction, poetry, and commentary on contemporary events, making it a valuable resource for studying colonial-era Korean intellectual history.

## Language Notes

The texts are written in Korean using a mix of:
- Hangul (Korean script)
- Hanja (Chinese characters)
- Historical orthography conventions

Researchers working with this corpus may need to account for historical spelling variations and mixed-script text.
